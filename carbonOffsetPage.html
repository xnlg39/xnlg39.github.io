Carbon offset

Some greenhouse emissions seem unavoidable, but is there a way to offset them? In this article, Ollie and Shiji explain what carbon offset is, why you should consider buying it and where to buy it.

By Shiji Qin and Ollie Campbell-lendrum

|

20 Jan 2021

|

5 min

The purchase of carbon offsets is an effective way for an individual to reduce the size of their carbon footprint without drastically changing their lifestyle. Carbon offsets allow people, companies, and countries to invest money into programs that reduce greenhouse gas production or increase carbon storage, with the aim of balancing out the emissions that they have previously made elsewhere. An example of when someone might want to purchase carbon offsets is in order to balance out the emissions produced on a long haul flight.


What is Carbon offset?


The most common method of buying carbon offsets is through the purchase of carbon credits. When purchased a carbon credit permits the bearer to cause the emission of one tonne of CO2 in exchange for a reduction of equivalent size elsewhere, this is mostly done by companies and countries to meet carbon neutrality commitments, but can also be done by individuals.

Carbon offset schemes come in many different forms, such as afforestation, investing in renewable energy schemes, or projects to increase energy efficiency.

Some of the most effective carbon offset schemes and have the most return on investment is those that lead to an increase in energy efficiency. Electronic appliances used in developing countries are not always the most efficient, so it is a relatively cheap fix for invested money to be used to swap these appliances for more energy efficient equivalents. For example, a low energy light bulb installed for six years can reduce carbon emissions by 250kg.


Why Buy Carbon Offsets?


In daily life, there are a lot of unavoidable carbon emissions, such as those caused by the production of phones and computers that we need to function in the modern world, or the fuel burned driving to work every day. People purchase carbon offsets to reduce their own carbon footprint and, in some ways, ease their own guilt, without having to make drastic lifestyle changes.

Take flying as an example, 4.5 billion passengers traveled by plane, in 2019, causing a total of 915 million tons of carbon emissions according to this article. Many of these passengers will feel guilty about the large amount of carbon dioxide produced by these flights, so will purchase carbon offsets to reduce the impact of their flight.

Where Can You Buy Carbon Offsets?


It is becoming increasingly easy for consumers to purchase carbon offsets, whether that is through the purchase of carbon credits or through direct investment in projects. Some companies operating in high polluting industries such as airlines, also make it possible for consumers to purchase carbon offsets when they buy their ticket.

Carbon Offsetting Criticisms


Whilst purchasing carbon offsets does help to reduce net carbon dioxide emissions and therefore slow down the rate of climate change, it is difficult to measure the effectiveness of an individual scheme particularly when it is an investment in a new technology that may not come to fruition. It is also argued that carbon offsets do not actually tackle the cause of global warming and instead allow people to continue polluting with a clear conscious. The environmentalist and writer George Monbiot likened the purchase of carbon offsets to the medieval act of indulging in your sins and paying the church afterwards to redeem yourselves.
